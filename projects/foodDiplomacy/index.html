From here, we can examine 3 themes that have emerged from our mapping: Acquired Taste & Consumer Health, Food Loss Prevention, and Farm Subsidies

The current food system in America has created a health apartheid driven by racial and class disparities.

Crop loss continues to be a major factor in the livelihood of small farmers. As the climate crisis drives more extreme weather, small farmers are increasingly vulnerable to climate shock. To mitigate these losses, farmers tend to overproduce, which keeps postharvest loss levels high. Tracking production levels and market demands can allocate the abundance.

Federal Agricultural Subsidies incentivize production of monocultures and processed foods. Farmers turn to synthetic fertilizers to maximize crop yields, producing emissions and driving acidification and desertification in the process.
